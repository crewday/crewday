An operations back-office for places with beds

Run your houses like a hotel. Without the front desk.

crew.day is the operations back-office for owners and small property-management outfits — designed so an LLM agent runs the day-to-day and the humans live in the house.

I
Agent-first

Every button is a tool call.

Every user-facing action in crew.day is also exposed as a CLI command; every CLI command is a tool the agent can call; every action is attributed to the delegating user and written to the audit log. The UI is a shell around those commands, not a separate capability.

High-impact actions still require a human's explicit approval — the agent drafts, you sign.

Fig. 2 — delegation
II
Places · People · Tasks

A ledger for who does what, where.

Properties, staff, stays, tasks, payroll — all modelled around the ways a small outfit actually works. Owners and managers see everything; workers see today; clients see the slice that is theirs. One data model, three audiences, no double-entry.

Turnovers auto-generate from bookings. Evidence photos close tasks. Hours roll up to payslips. The boring parts stay boring.

Fig. 3 — ledger
III
Self-hostable, single-binary

Your box, your data. Your keys.

One binary, one SQLite file. Postgres optional. No required SaaS, no phone-home, no marketplace you have to use. The managed deployment at crew.day is convenience — not a gate — and every feature of the product is available on the box sitting in your hall cupboard.

Time is UTC at rest, local for display. No PII goes to an upstream LLM without explicit opt-in.
